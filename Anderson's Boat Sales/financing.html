How boat financing works at Anderson's

Submit one application and Anderson's shops it to a panel of marine-specific lenders. Most applicants have a decision within 24 hours, and terms run from 60 to 240 months depending on the amount financed.

Marine lenders differ from auto lenders: they underwrite the hull as an asset, they allow longer terms, and they finance the trailer, electronics and extended coverage in the same contract. Anderson's finance office is on-site at both Michigan stores, so the loan, the title work and the trade payoff are handled at one desk on the day you take delivery.

Before you apply

What to have ready

  • Driver's license and Social Security number
  • Two most recent pay stubs, or two years of returns if self-employed
  • Proof of residence — a utility bill works
  • Trade-in title or lender payoff letter
  • Down payment source: cash, trade equity, or both

Applying with a co-borrower? Bring the same set for them.

Answers

Boat financing, answered

Most marine lenders approve applicants at 680 and above for standard rates, with the best pricing above 740. Anderson's works with lenders who write below 680 as well, typically with a larger down payment. There is no minimum score to apply and a soft pre-qualification does not affect your credit.

Terms run from 60 to 240 months. The maximum term depends on the amount financed and the hull age — larger, newer boats qualify for the longest terms, while smaller or older hulls are capped shorter.

Ten to twenty percent is typical. More money down lowers your rate and your payment, and trade equity counts toward the down payment — bring a payoff letter and we'll fold it into the deal.

Yes. Pre-owned hulls typically add half a point to a point and a half over new pricing and carry a shorter maximum term, but our lender panel writes used-boat paper every day.

If your boat has a galley, a head and a berth, it may qualify as a second home, making the loan interest potentially deductible. Talk to your tax advisor — we can supply the year-end interest statement.

Marine lenders let you roll the trailer, electronics and extended coverage into the same contract as the boat, so it's all one payment instead of several.
